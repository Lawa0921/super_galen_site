Character background

A poster in the U.S. rewrote my map.

Finishing my master's in Taiwan, I stood where a lot of us stand: keep going for a PhD, or step into the biotech industry? I didn't have the answer yet.

In April 2024 I flew to the AACR meeting in the States with my advisor and labmates to present my thesis as a poster — I treated it as a quiet graduation gift to myself. Somewhere between conversations at other groups' posters, I walked away with a job offer I never planned for.

So I took it, packed up, and crossed the Pacific to find out what I was made of.

Tampa runs at a different tempo. I learned to hold several experiments in my head at once, to troubleshoot alone, and to redraw the workflow the moment conditions shifted. Along the way I built my own way of working at the bench — and I'm still adding to the skill tree every single week.
